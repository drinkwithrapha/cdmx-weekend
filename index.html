Mondrian by one, tacos by one-fifteen, anthropology until close.

Practical notes.

Altitude

7,350 ft. Hydrate hard the first day, ease off the mezcal on Friday night, don't be surprised if Bellas Artes stairs feel steeper than they are.

Transport

Uber and Didi. Cheap, safe, 15–25 min between any two neighborhoods on the plan. Centro is a straight shot up Av. Cuauhtémoc from Condesa.

Cash

Hidalguense, Los Cocuyos, Los Parados, and the street stands prefer cash. Keep 1,500 MXN in smaller bills for the weekend.

Wine angle

Rosetta runs a serious natural-leaning list with strong Mexican representation — Bichi (Valle de Guadalupe), Cuna de Tierra (Guanajuato), and Vinisterra are the names to ask after. Café Tacobar is the mezcal counterweight: ask Chris for a pour from the back shelf, not the menu.

Rain contingency

Small afternoon shower risk Saturday. Bellas Artes, Torre Latino, and Casa de los Azulejos sit within a three-block covered radius — the Centro stops bunch well if it breaks.

Per-person budget

Museums & views ~820 MXN. Airport + in-city Ubers ~2,000 MXN. Food ranges from ~4,500 MXN (taco-heavy, modest drinks) to ~8,000 MXN (full wine service at Rosetta, extended mezcal at Café Tacobar). Weekend total: ~7,500–11,000 MXN per person, roughly $375–550 USD. Swap wine for mezcal and you land at the lower end.
